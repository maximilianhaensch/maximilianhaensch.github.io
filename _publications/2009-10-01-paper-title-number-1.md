Abstract: We study positivity properties of exact observables in planar N=4 super Yang-Mills as functions of the 't Hooft coupling. Motivated by analogous results in quantum mechanics, we ask whether such observables admit a once-subtracted dispersion representation in the coupling over a positive spectral measure. Our main result is that this property, also known as the Stieltjes property, holds for a broad class of exact observables. We prove it analytically, through integral representations, for the octagon anomalous dimension, the logarithm of the circular Wilson loop, the Bremsstrahlung function, and anomalous dimensions in the BMN limit, and we provide numerical evidence for the cusp and tilted cusp anomalous dimensions. We also identify quantities for which the Stieltjes property does not hold, and study the weaker positivity property of complete monotonicity. The Stieltjes property yields two powerful consequences: it lets us turn perturbative input into rigorous non-perturbative bounds, and bootstrap perturbative coefficients. We also show how the strong-coupling expansion and its non-perturbative corrections can be recovered from the once-subtracted dispersion representation via a Mellin-Barnes representation and outline a method to estimate the strong-coupling expansion from weak-coupling data.
